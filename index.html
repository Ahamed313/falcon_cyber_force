Penetration Tester

Penetration Tester A dedicated and ethical Penetration Tester responsible for identifying, exploiting, and reporting security vulnerabilities across web applications, networks, and systems. He specializes in simulating real-world cyberattacks to evaluate the effectiveness of security controls and uncover weaknesses before they can be exploited by malicious actors. With a strong understanding of networking, operating systems, and application security, he combines automated tools with manual testing techniques to deliver accurate and actionable security assessments. He produces clear, professional reports that outline risk severity, technical findings, and practical remediation steps. He works closely with developers, SOC teams, and system administrators to strengthen defenses, validate fixes, and continuously improve the organization’s security posture. Committed to ethical standards and continuous learning, he stays up-to-date with emerging threats, attack methodologies, and industry best practices.

SOC Engineer

SOC Engineer A skilled SOC Engineer responsible for designing, managing, and optimizing security monitoring infrastructure within the Security Operations Center (SOC). He focuses on building and maintaining SIEM platforms, log pipelines, and detection mechanisms to ensure continuous visibility across systems, networks, and applications. He develops and tunes detection rules, correlation logic, and alerts to accurately identify suspicious activities while reducing false positives. By integrating multiple security tools and data sources, he enables efficient threat detection, investigation, and response workflows. The SOC Engineer works closely with SOC analysts and incident response teams to support real-time investigations and automate repetitive security tasks. He continuously improves monitoring capabilities, enhances security automation, and ensures the SOC environment remains scalable, reliable, and resilient against evolving cyber threats. Committed to operational excellence and continuous improvement, he stays updated with the latest detection techniques, threat intelligence, and SOC best practices to strengthen the organization’s overall security posture.

Threat Intelligence Researcher

Threat Intelligence Researcher A dedicated Threat Intelligence Researcher responsible for tracking, analyzing, and interpreting cyber threat data to help the organization stay ahead of emerging attacks. He monitors malware campaigns, attacker techniques, and global threat trends to provide actionable intelligence to the security team. He collects data from multiple sources, including open-source intelligence (OSINT), dark web monitoring, and internal security logs. Using advanced analytical techniques, he identifies potential threats, assesses their impact, and advises SOC and incident response teams on preventive measures. The Threat Intelligence Researcher collaborates with cybersecurity analysts, penetration testers, and incident response specialists to enhance detection capabilities, develop threat-hunting strategies, and strengthen overall security posture. He continuously updates his knowledge of threat actor behavior, attack methodologies, and cybersecurity best practices to ensure proactive defense against evolving cyber threats.

Incident Response Specialist

Incident Response Specialist An experienced Incident Response Specialist responsible for leading the detection, containment, and resolution of cybersecurity incidents. He acts quickly to minimize damage, restore systems, and ensure business continuity during security breaches. He investigates security alerts, analyzes attack vectors, and determines the scope and impact of incidents. By coordinating with SOC teams, penetration testers, and system administrators, he ensures timely and effective response to threats. The Incident Response Specialist also conducts post-incident reviews, documenting lessons learned and recommending improvements to prevent future attacks. Committed to continuous improvement, he stays updated on emerging threats, attack techniques, and industry best practices to strengthen the organization’s overall security posture.
